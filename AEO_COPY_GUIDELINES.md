Adopt the "Inverted Pyramid" and Answer-First Structure Place the most vital information at the very beginning of your content
. Every H2 or H3 section should immediately open with a direct, concise 1-2 sentence "answer capsule" of around 40–60 words
. Once you have provided this definitive answer, you can follow it up with deeper context, supporting details, and examples.
Use Question-Driven Headings Structure your copy around real questions that users actually ask. Format your H2 and H3 tags to mirror natural, conversational queries. For example, instead of a vague heading like "Side Effects," use a specific question like "Does Retinol Cause Skin Irritation?". This helps AI algorithms instantly match user intent to your content.
Write for Extractability with Clear Formatting Answer engines strongly prefer content that is structured, modular, and scannable. Break up large blocks of text by incorporating bullet points, numbered lists, and tables, which are highly favored by AI models for zero-click answers. Keep your paragraphs "atomic," ideally sticking to 1 to 3 short sentences to make it easy for an AI to parse and extract your information cleanly.
Maintain a Conversational yet Definitive Tone Write using simple, clear, natural language that aligns with how people speak to voice assistants. However, eliminate marketing fluff and ambiguous phrasing. AI systems are risk-averse and avoid citing vague language like "Some experts say..." or "Many believe that...". Instead, use factual, declarative, and confident statements. Use semantic cues like "According to," "For example," or "In contrast" to help the AI understand the relationship between your sentences.
Provide "Information Gain" and Strong E-E-A-T Signals Because AI models are trained on vast amounts of existing data, your copy must offer unique value—known as "Information Gain"—to be deemed citation-worthy.
Inject original research, proprietary data, real-world case studies, and first-hand experiences into your copy.
Transparently back up your claims by citing authoritative external sources.
Attribute your content to named authors with verifiable expertise and bios.
Use Consistent Entity Terminology AI models process information by mapping relationships between entities (people, places, concepts, things). Always use precise and consistent names for tools, products, and concepts throughout your copy. For example, consistently use "Google AI Overviews" rather than alternating with vague terms like "Google's new AI thing" or "AI summaries" to ensure the AI correctly categorizes your text.
Keep the Copy Visibly Fresh AI engines prioritize up-to-date, relevant answers. Regularly audit and refresh your copy by updating outdated statistics, removing deprecated tools, and adding recent developments. Prominently display a "Last updated" timestamp at the top of your content so AI crawlers can easily verify its recency.
